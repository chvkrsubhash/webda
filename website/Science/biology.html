Biology

Biology is the scientific study of life. It is a natural science with a broad scope but has several unifying themes that tie it together as a single, coherent field. For instance, all organisms are made up of cells that process hereditary information encoded in genes, which can be transmitted to future generations.

Key Concepts in Biology

  • Life: Life is a complex and dynamic process that is characterized by several key features, including the ability to reproduce, grow, and adapt to the environment.
  • Cells: Cells are the basic units of life. All organisms are made up of cells, and cells are the smallest units that can carry out the functions of life.
  • Genes: Genes are the units of heredity. They are passed from parents to offspring and contain the instructions for building and maintaining an organism.
  • Evolution: Evolution is the process of change over time. It is the driving force behind the diversity of life on Earth.
  • Energy: Energy is essential for life. Organisms must obtain energy from their environment in order to survive and reproduce.
  • Homeostasis: Homeostasis is the ability of an organism to maintain a stable internal environment. This is necessary for the organism to function properly.

Biology is a fascinating and important field of science. It is used to understand the world around us and to develop new technologies.
