Geography

Geography is the study of the Earth and its human and natural features. It is a broad discipline that encompasses many different subfields, including physical geography, human geography, and environmental geography.

Subfields of Geography

  • Physical geography is the study of the physical features of the Earth, such as its landforms, climate, and oceans.
  • Human geography is the study of the human societies that inhabit the Earth, such as their cultures, economies, and politics.
  • Environmental geography is the study of the interactions between human societies and the natural environment.

Geographers use a variety of methods to study the Earth, including maps, satellite imagery, and field research. They also use a variety of theories and concepts to understand the relationships between different aspects of the Earth system.
