Electrical Engineering

Electrical engineering is a broad engineering discipline that deals with the study, design, development, and application of electrical systems, equipment, and devices. Electrical engineers work in a wide range of industries, including power generation and distribution, telecommunications, electronics, and automation.

Subfields of Electrical Engineering

  • Power engineering: This subfield deals with the generation, transmission, and distribution of electrical power.
  • Electronics engineering: This subfield deals with the design and development of electronic devices and circuits.
  • Control engineering: This subfield deals with the design and development of systems that control the behavior of other systems.
  • Signal processing: This subfield deals with the analysis and processing of electrical signals.
  • Computer engineering: This subfield deals with the design and development of computer hardware and software.

Benefits of a Career in Electrical Engineering

  • High salary: Electrical engineers earn a median annual salary of $91,130.
  • Job security: The job outlook for electrical engineers is good.
  • Variety of opportunities: Electrical engineers can work in a variety of industries, including power generation, telecommunications, electronics, and automation.
  • Chance to make a difference: Electrical engineers work on projects that can have a positive impact on society, such as developing new technologies for energy efficiency and renewable energy.
